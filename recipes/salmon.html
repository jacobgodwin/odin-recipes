Mayo-Seared Salmon

Description

Perfect, flaky salmon that is seared with a mayo marinade, and then finished in the oven.

The mayo-marinade provides a binder for spices to stick to the salmon and is used to get nice brown crust without the need for extra oil in the pan.

Ingredients

  • 2 Salmon Fillets
  • Mayonnaise
  • Kosher Salt
  • Pepper
  • Oregano
  • Lemon
  • Sugar

Steps

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 400F
  2. Brine the salmon by combining 40g of kosher salt and 10g of sugar in a bowl. Pour the mixture over the salmon and coat the flesh of the fish. Let it sit in the fridge for about 30 minutes. This will help firm up the skin for cooking and provide some seasoning to the fish.
  3. While the salmon is dry brining, measure out 1.5 TBSP of mayo in a bowl along with the zest of 1 lemon, .5 TBSP of oregano, and a few cranks of cracked, black pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. After the 30 minute dry brine is done, rinse the salmon with cold water and pat dry with a paper towel.
  5. Season the salmon with kosher salt and pepper. Then smear the mayo-marinade over the flesh of the salmon.
  6. Heat a cast-iron skillet or oven-safe skillet over medium to medium-high heat. We want this pretty hot to get a nice sear on the flesh as we'll be finishing it off in the oven.
  7. Once the pan is hot, place the salmon, skin-side down, in the pan and sear for about 2 - 4 minutes checking to make sure the flesh doesn't burn.
  8. Once a nice golden brown sear is achieved, flip the salmon over and crisp up the skin for a minute and then toss the pan in the oven for 6 - 10 minutes depending on the thickness of the salmon and your preferred doneness. (I like to aim for 125F - 135F internal temperature for a moist and flaky salmon)
